# Plan: Batalha Total Victory (Miniature Combat System)

## 1. Core Concept
A modular sourcebook for 3D&T Victory focused entirely on tactical miniature and grid-based combat. This book will provide game masters and players with a robust toolbox to simulate conflicts of varying sizes, from small skirmishes to massive wars. It is designed to be setting-agnostic, with rules applicable to fantasy, sci-fi, modern, or any other campaign.

## 2. Key Systems & Mechanics

### a. Tactical Grid Combat
- **Movement & Positioning:** Formal rules for grid-based movement (squares or hexes), including clear definitions for movement speed, diagonal moves, and flanking.
- **Action Economy:** Refined action system for tactical play (e.g., Move Action, Standard Action, Swift Action) to create dynamic choices in combat.
- **Cover & Terrain:** Simple, impactful rules.
    - **Light Cover (e.g., a crate, tree):** +1 to Defense.
    - **Heavy Cover (e.g., bunker wall, fortified position):** +2 to Defense, may block line of sight.
    - **Difficult Terrain:** Costs double movement to cross.
    - **Destructible Terrain:** Rules for powerful attacks to alter the battlefield.

### b. Squads & Formations
- **The Squad Unit:** Rules for treating a group of 3-5 Ningen-scale NPCs as a single unit with a shared initiative and hit point pool (building on the "Mob" advantage).
- **Squad Actions:** Special actions available only to squads.
    - **Volley Fire:** The squad focuses fire, granting a bonus to hit a single target.
    - **Suppressing Fire:** The squad pins down an area, forcing enemies who move through it to make a resistance test or lose their action.
    - **Form Wall:** The squad provides heavy cover to characters behind them.

### c. The System of Diverse Scales
This is the core of the book, detailing how different scales interact on the battlefield.

- **Ningen (Human Scale):** The baseline. Represents individual soldiers, heroes, or adventurers.
    - *Interaction:* The standard for combat.

- **Sugoi (Superior Scale):** Represents elite warriors, light vehicles, cavalry, or large beasts like ogres.
    - *Interaction:* Gain a "Ganho" (advantage die) on all actions against Ningen scale. Can move through Ningen spaces (risking a counter-attack). Can use a "Ram" action to knock down and damage Ningen units.

- **Kiodai (Giant Scale):** Represents tanks, mechs, giants, and dragons.
    - *Interaction:* Automatically succeed on attacks against Ningen scale (dealing damage to a whole squad/mob at once). Have automatic criticals against Sugoi scale. Their missed shots can become area-of-effect attacks that damage the terrain. Cannot be harmed by most Ningen-scale attacks except with special "Siege" weapons or by targeting specific weak points (a new mechanic).

- **Kami (God Scale):** Represents colossal capital ships, kaiju, or mobile fortresses. These are often treated as the battlefield itself.
    - *Interaction:* Cannot be targeted or harmed by conventional attacks from lower scales. They are defeated by completing scenario objectives (e.g., destroying their power core [Kiodai], disabling their main weapon [Kiodai], or assassinating their pilot [Ningen]). Their attacks are telegraphed, map-altering events that GMs announce a round in advance.

## 3. Modular Toolbox for GMs
- **Point-Buy Creation:** A system for GMs to build their own vehicles, monsters, and squads. Points are used to buy Scale, stats (P,H,R), and special abilities (Flight, Siege Weapon, Amphibious, etc.).
- **Scenario Generator:** Tables and guides for creating balanced combat encounters with clear objectives beyond "defeat all enemies" (e.g., King of the Hill, Capture the Flag, Escort the VIP, Survive X Rounds).

## 4. Proposed Book Structure
1.  **Introduction:** Wargaming with 3D&T Victory.
2.  **Chapter 1: The Tactical Grid:** Rules for movement, actions, and terrain.
3.  **Chapter 2: Squads & Companies:** Creating and controlling groups of soldiers.
4.  **Chapter 3: The Scale of War:** The definitive rules for how Ningen, Sugoi, Kiodai, and Kami interact.
5.  **Chapter 4: Engines of Destruction:** A toolbox for creating vehicles, monsters, and other large-scale threats.
6.  **Chapter 5: Running the Battle:** How to GM tactical combat, with scenario objectives, reinforcements, and morale rules.
7.  **Appendix:** Pre-built example units for Fantasy, Sci-Fi, and Modern settings to get started quickly.
